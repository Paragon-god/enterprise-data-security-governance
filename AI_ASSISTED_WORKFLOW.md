# AI Assisted Workflow and Human Validation

## Purpose

This document explains how AI tools supported the project while preserving human accountability.

## AI supported activities

AI tools supported:

1. Project planning and sequencing.
2. Documentation drafting and refinement.
3. Spreadsheet and register generation.
4. Code organisation and troubleshooting.
5. Control mapping and consistency review.
6. Sanitisation planning.
7. Portfolio and launch material preparation.

## Human controlled activities

The portfolio owner retained responsibility for:

1. Environment and account access.
2. Dataset handling.
3. Technical execution.
4. Screenshot capture.
5. Validation of results.
6. Review of quantitative claims.
7. Risk and control decisions.
8. Approval of public evidence.
9. Final publication.

## Validation method

AI generated material was treated as draft content.

Claims were checked against:

* actual execution output;
* stored screenshots;
* model metrics;
* database results;
* architecture evidence; and
* governance registers.

Material that could not be supported was labelled as a recommendation rather than an implemented control.

## Disclosure principle

AI use is disclosed as part of the project governance practice. It is not hidden from reviewers, and it does not replace human responsibility for accuracy or publication.
